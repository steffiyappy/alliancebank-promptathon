🏦Department Context

Alliance Bank's SME Banking division is a cornerstone of the Acceler8 2027 strategy, targeting Malaysia's 1.2 million SME businesses. Relationship Managers (RMs) serve a portfolio of clients across manufacturing, retail, services, and construction, providing working capital loans, trade finance, and cash management solutions.

⚠️The Business Pain Point

SME loan applications at Alliance Bank take an average of 14 working days from application submission to approval decision. Digital-native competitors like CIMB BizChannel and GXBank promise 48-hour approvals. The bottleneck is not risk appetite — it's a manual process: RMs compile documents by email, credit memos are typed from scratch for every application, approval chains require 3-4 sign-offs via email, and exception handling is ad hoc. The result: Alliance Bank loses SME deals to faster competitors, and RMs spend 40% of their time on paperwork instead of growing their portfolio.

🎯Your Challenge

Complete all three tasks using Microsoft 365 Copilot. Document your prompts and outputs in your team's working file.

  1. Task 1: Use Copilot Chat to map out the current 14-day loan approval process step by step, then identify at least 3 specific bottlenecks where Copilot could reduce time or manual effort. Be specific about which Copilot tool would be used at each bottleneck.

  2. Task 2: Use Copilot in Word to draft a standardised SME Credit Memo template that an RM can populate using voice or typed inputs, and have Copilot structure it automatically — covering loan purpose, business background, financial summary, risk factors, and recommendation.

  3. Task 3: Use Copilot in PowerPoint to create a 3-slide proposal to the Head of SME Banking showing: current state vs. desired state, your recommended Copilot-enabled process changes, and projected time savings.

🛠️Recommended Copilot Tools

Copilot Chat — process bottleneck analysis
Copilot in Word — credit memo drafting
Copilot in Outlook — approval chain communication
Copilot in PowerPoint — proposal narrative

📦What to Submit

Your team must present these deliverables during the judging session:

Process bottleneck analysis (Chat output) SME Credit Memo template (Word) 3-slide proposal (PowerPoint) Sample RM email chain (optional)

🎲 Game Master Guide

This scenario works best with Copilot Chat (mapping the approval process and identifying waste), Copilot in Word (drafting the credit memo template), and optionally Copilot Agent Builder (an application-status agent that RMs can query). The Power Ups "Action Plan in 5 Steps" and "Check for Blind Spots" are particularly strong here — they push teams to think about change management, not just process design.

⚡ Your Power Ups

These are prompt modifiers. Take your best Copilot output from the challenge and apply one (or more) of these to make it even stronger.

01 Action Plan in 5 Steps
"Rewrite your Copilot-enabled process changes as 5 concrete actions with owners, timelines, and success metrics."
Apply this to: your 3-slide proposal to give leadership a clear, executable roadmap.
02 Check for Blind Spots
"Ask Copilot: what could go wrong with this approach? What have we not considered — from RM resistance, to data quality, to regulatory constraints?"
Apply this to: your bottleneck analysis to stress-test your recommendations before presenting.
03 Next 90 Days Plan
"Turn your proposal into a 90-day phased implementation plan: what happens in month 1, month 2, and month 3? What does success look like at the end of 90 days?"
Apply this to: your PowerPoint proposal slide 3 to add a credible implementation timeline.
04 Turn into KPIs Table
"Convert your expected improvements into a KPI table with metric name, current baseline, 90-day target, owner, and measurement method."
Apply this to: your process savings projections to make the business case concrete and measurable.
