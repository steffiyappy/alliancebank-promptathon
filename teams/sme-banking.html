🏦About Aurora Nexus Global

Aurora Nexus Global is a mid-sized financial services group operating across Malaysia and the APAC region. The SME Banking division serves thousands of small and medium businesses across manufacturing, retail, services, and construction, providing working capital loans, trade finance, and cash management. SME lending is the cornerstone of Project Nexus 2027 — but the process meant to win business clients is, in practice, driving them away.

⚠️Business Pain Point

Aurora Nexus Global's target turnaround for SME loan decisions is 5 working days. The reality is 14. For a business owner waiting on a working capital line to pay salaries or secure a contract, that gap is the difference between staying with ANG and moving to a competitor.

The bottleneck is not credit risk — ANG's approval rates are healthy. It is the process. Relationship Managers manually compile documents from customers, type credit memos from scratch for every application, chase internal approvals over email, and follow up on missing documents through WhatsApp. Every step is manual, every step introduces delay, and every step pulls RMs away from their core job: growing client relationships.

Two large manufacturing clients cited "slow response compared to competitors" when moving their facilities in Q1 2025. RMs are aware of the problem. Leadership is aware of the problem. But no one has systematically mapped where the time actually goes — or made it someone's job to fix it.

📧Email Thread

From: David Loh, Head of SME Banking — Aurora Nexus Global
To: Regional SME Managers
Subject: We Are Losing Deals We Should Be Winning

I received confirmation this week that one of our anchor manufacturing clients — a relationship we have held since 2019 — has moved their facility to a competitor. Their feedback: "ANG is too slow." We were their first call. We were not their last answer.

This is not a pricing problem. Our rates are competitive. This is a process problem. I've asked operations to pull the Q1 application log. I want every RM to examine their own applications and identify where we lost days we should not have lost. We cannot win the SME segment with a 14-day process when competitors are promising 48 hours.

🎯Your Tasks

1

Based on the application log and team conversations, identify the stages where Aurora Nexus Global is losing the most time — and distinguish between delays caused by process design versus delays caused by customer behaviour.

2

Design an ideal SME loan journey — from the moment an RM receives an application to the moment a decision is communicated — that removes unnecessary manual steps without compromising credit quality.

3

Recommend what Aurora Nexus Global must change first to move from a 14-day average toward a 5-day target, and what success would look like 90 days after those changes are made.
