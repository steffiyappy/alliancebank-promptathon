🏦About Aurora Nexus Global

Aurora Nexus Global is a mid-sized financial services group operating across Malaysia and the APAC region. The Strategy & Transformation division drives the execution of Project Nexus 2027, the Group's transformation strategy spanning 8 pillars and 47 cross-functional initiatives. Eighteen months in, the strategy is visible on every presentation. What is becoming invisible is whether it is actually working.

⚠️Business Pain Point

Project Nexus 2027 was designed for accountability. Each pillar has an executive sponsor. Each initiative has a stated owner. Quarterly Business Reviews are held with all division heads. On paper, governance is clear.

In practice, the Q2 2025 Quarterly Business Review told a different story. Of 47 active initiatives, 28 had no updated milestone status. Eleven had changed owners without any formal update to the tracker. Six had been quietly de-scoped without Board notification. The Strategy team spent three weeks preparing the QBR pack, and the CEO's first question in the meeting was whether the data could be trusted.

The deeper problem is that accountability for Project Nexus has drifted. Initiative owners treat the strategy tracker as a reporting obligation, not a management tool. When initiatives fall behind, the flag goes up in the QBR, three months after the slip began. By then, it is too late to recover the quarter.

📧Email Thread

From: Group CEO, Aurora Nexus Global
To: Group Strategy & Transformation
Subject: QBR Preparation: I Need to Trust the Numbers

I've reviewed the draft QBR pack. My concern is not the number of initiatives at risk; some slippage in a transformation programme is expected. My concern is that I cannot tell, from this pack, whether the people responsible for these initiatives are genuinely managing them or simply submitting status updates.

Three initiatives show "At Risk" with last updated dates from February. We are in May. Either nothing has changed in three months, which is a problem in itself, or things have changed and no one updated the tracker. Either way, I'm walking into a Board presentation without confidence in the data. I need the strategy team to solve this. Not just for this quarter. Permanently.
