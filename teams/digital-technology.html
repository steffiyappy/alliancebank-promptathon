🏦Department Context

Alliance Bank's Digital Banking & Technology division powers allianceonline (internet banking), the Alliance Bank mobile app, and the Group's internal technology infrastructure. The division is central to the Acceler8 2027 digital leadership pillar, responsible for delivering new features, improving cybersecurity posture, and managing the technology stack across branches, contact centres, and back-office systems.

⚠️The Business Pain Point

Alliance Bank's digital banking platform targets an 8-week release cycle for new features and improvements. But in practice, each release is delayed by 2-3 weeks on average — not due to coding problems, but due to documentation and coordination failures. Business Analysts (BAs) write requirements in inconsistent formats; Product Owners and developers interpret them differently, requiring multiple rounds of clarification. UAT tracking is done in shared Excel spreadsheets that teams overwrite. Post-release incident reports take 3-4 days to write, delaying the next sprint. Senior developers complain that 35% of their time is spent clarifying requirements or writing documentation rather than coding.

🎯Your Challenge

Complete all three tasks using Microsoft 365 Copilot. Document your prompts and outputs in your team's working file.

  1. Task 1: Use Copilot Chat to review this sample poorly-written business requirement and rewrite it as a clear, unambiguous user story with acceptance criteria. Poor requirement: "The login page should be better and users should be able to log in easier especially on mobile and we should add some security stuff." Copilot should produce a properly structured user story with: persona, goal, value statement, and at least 4 testable acceptance criteria.

  2. Task 2: Use Copilot in Word to create a Business Requirements Document (BRD) template for Alliance Bank's digital team — with sections for business objective, user stories, functional requirements, non-functional requirements, assumptions, and out of scope. Include example content for a "biometric login" feature.

  3. Task 3: Use Copilot in PowerPoint to create a 3-slide technology leadership update showing: the release delay problem, the proposed documentation-quality improvements powered by Copilot, and the expected impact on delivery speed and developer satisfaction.

🎲 Game Master Guide

This scenario works best with Copilot Chat (rewriting and improving requirements), Copilot in Word (creating structured documentation templates), and Copilot Agent Builder (a "release readiness" agent that checks requirement quality before sprint planning). Teams should focus on quality gates, not just speed — the goal is reducing rework. The Power Ups "Convert to SOP", "Action Plan in 5 Steps", and "What If Analysis" are particularly strong here.

⚡ Your Power Ups

These are prompt modifiers. Take your best Copilot output from the challenge and apply one (or more) of these to make it even stronger.

01 Convert to SOP
"Turn your BRD template into a step-by-step SOP for Business Analysts: how to write a high-quality requirements document that developers can use without clarification."

02 Action Plan in 5 Steps
"Rewrite your Copilot-enabled documentation improvements as 5 concrete actions with owners, timelines, and what 'done' looks like for each."

03 What If Analysis
"Explore: what if every Business Requirement goes through a Copilot quality check before it enters the sprint backlog? Show the impact on delay rates, developer satisfaction, and release velocity."

"Ask Copilot: what could go wrong with AI-assisted requirements writing? What are the risks of over-relying on Copilot for documentation quality?"
Apply this to: your leadership update to show you've thought through risks, not just upside.
