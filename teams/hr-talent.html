🏦About Aurora Nexus Global

Aurora Nexus Global is a mid-sized financial services group operating across Malaysia and the APAC region. The HR & Talent division manages the professional development and engagement of over 3,000 employees across the Group. Under Project Nexus 2027, HR is responsible for building a workforce ready for transformation, but the current training model is producing completion records, not capability.

⚠️Business Pain Point

Aurora Nexus Global measures training success by completion rate. On paper, 89% of staff have completed their mandatory modules this year. In practice, the same compliance questions land in HR inboxes every week: questions about policies that were "covered" in training months ago.

The problem is structural. Training content is static. It was designed to be completed, not to be used. Employees click through slides, pass the end quiz with multiple attempts, and move on. When they face a real situation, whether a leave dispute, a regulatory question, or an escalation, the training offers no guidance because it was never built for the moment of need.

HR Business Partners estimate they spend 60% of their time answering individual queries that training was supposed to address. Meanwhile, policy documents are scattered across the LMS, SharePoint, PDF attachments, and old email chains. When a new regulatory requirement was issued in March 2025, it took 11 weeks before updated training reached all staff.

📧Email Thread

From: Tengku Aliff, HR Business Partner, Consumer Banking & SME, Aurora Nexus Global
To: Head of HR & Talent
Subject: We Are Answering the Same Questions We Trained People On

I spent three and a half hours yesterday on HR queries. Six were about AML thresholds, all covered in the March training module. Two more were about leave carry-over, which is clearly stated in the January policy update.

The problem is not that people are ignoring training. They complete it. The problem is that when they actually need the answer, in the middle of a customer interaction or preparing a report, they cannot retrieve it. A 40-slide deck from six months ago is not accessible in the moment of need. I'm not sure more training is the answer. I think we need to meet people where they are, when they need it.
