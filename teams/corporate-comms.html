🏦Department Context

Alliance Bank's Corporate Communications & Investor Relations division manages the Group's external and internal communications — including press releases, social media, investor announcements, regulatory disclosures, staff emails, and branch communications. The team works closely with the CEO office, Finance, Risk & Compliance, and all business units to ensure consistent, accurate, and on-brand messaging across all channels.

⚠️The Business Pain Point

Alliance Bank communicates through at least 8 distinct channels and 5 different content-producing teams, none of whom operate with a shared messaging playbook. When a sensitive event occurs — an interest rate change, a regulatory disclosure, an executive announcement — multiple teams independently draft responses within hours. The result is contradictory messaging: the press release says one thing, the staff circular says another, and social media takes a different angle entirely. This has led to three incidents in the past 12 months where journalists highlighted inconsistencies between Alliance Bank's official statements. The Communications team is reactive, not strategic, and brand trust scores have declined 6 points.

🎯Your Challenge

Complete all three tasks using Microsoft 365 Copilot. Document your prompts and outputs in your team's working file.

  1. Task 1: Use Copilot Chat to analyse the following three sample communications about the same event (an interest rate change) and identify: inconsistencies, missing information, tone differences, and recommended unified messaging. Sample 1 (Press Release): "Alliance Bank today confirmed an adjustment to home loan rates effective 1 June, aligned with market conditions." Sample 2 (Staff Email): "As you may have heard, we're changing our lending rates next month. Please update your customer conversations accordingly." Sample 3 (Social Media): "Exciting update! We're refreshing our home loan rates to better serve you 🏠✨ Stay tuned for details." Copilot should identify inconsistencies and produce a unified key messages document.

  2. Task 2: Use Copilot in Word to create an Alliance Bank Communications Playbook template — a 1-page rapid-response framework that any team can use when drafting communications about a sensitive event. Include: event classification (minor/moderate/high), key stakeholders to inform, tone guidelines, and approval workflow.

  3. Task 3: Use Copilot in PowerPoint to create a 3-slide proposal to the CEO showing: the messaging inconsistency problem, your proposed "Comms Guardian" framework (the playbook + a Copilot review step), and the expected impact on brand consistency and response time.

🎲 Game Master Guide

This scenario works best with Copilot in Word (drafting and aligning communications), Copilot Chat (identifying tone and factual inconsistencies), and Copilot Agent Builder (designing a "Comms Guardian" agent that surfaces approved messaging and flags drift). Participants should focus on context control and narrative alignment — not content creation speed. The Power Ups "Reframe for C-Suite", "Policy Draft", and "Escalation Brief" are most effective here.
