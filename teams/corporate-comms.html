🏦About Aurora Nexus Global

Aurora Nexus Global is a mid-sized financial services group operating across Malaysia and the APAC region. The Corporate Communications & Investor Relations division manages all external and internal messaging — press releases, investor updates, regulatory disclosures, social media, staff circulars, and branch communications. It is the voice of the Group, and right now it is speaking in too many conflicting accents.

⚠️Business Pain Point

Aurora Nexus Global communicates through at least eight channels, managed by five different teams who operate without a shared messaging playbook. Each team — Corporate Comms, Investor Relations, Marketing, Branch Communications, and HR — produces content independently, applying its own interpretation of ANG's tone, priorities, and key messages.

This works well enough on routine days. It breaks down when something sensitive happens. When ANG announced a revision to its home loan base rate in March 2025, four separate communications went out within two hours. The press release focused on competitive positioning. The investor announcement emphasised margin management. The staff circular told branches to expect customer questions. The social media post — sent by Marketing without sign-off — led with "exciting news for homeowners." A financial journalist called the Communications head within 90 minutes, asking which framing was accurate.

This has happened three times in the past 12 months. Each time, the root cause is the same: no one owns the master message, and no process exists to synchronise communications before they go out. The Group is not seen as speaking with one voice — and in a regulated financial services environment, that perception carries real consequence.

I read the article this morning. The journalist quoted three things ANG said within two hours — and all three were accurate quotes from our own communications. The problem is they contradict each other on framing.

We look like we do not have a point of view on our own decisions. In a regulated industry where trust is everything, that perception has consequences — with regulators, with investors, and with our own staff who do not know which version to believe. I want to understand how this happened and what changes. Not a post-mortem. An actual fix.
