🏦Department Context

Alliance Bank's Finance division manages Group financial planning, performance reporting, and Board-level financial communication. Led by the CFO, the team is responsible for the monthly financial close, budget management, variance analysis, and producing the Board pack — a critical document reviewed by Alliance Bank's Board of Directors every month.

⚠️The Business Pain Point

Every month, Alliance Bank's finance team runs what internally they call "the Excel Marathon" — a 5-day period at month-end where analysts manually collect, consolidate, and reconcile 23 Excel files submitted by different business units. By the time the data is clean, analysts have only 1 day left to produce actual insights and build the Board narrative. The result: 60% of analyst time is spent on data gathering, not analysis. Narrative explanations in the Board pack vary wildly in quality between business units. And when a number changes in one file, cascading updates across 23 files take hours. Two analysts have already left citing "Excel fatigue."

🎯Your Challenge

Complete all three tasks using Microsoft 365 Copilot. Document your prompts and outputs in your team's working file.

  1. Task 1: Use Copilot Chat to analyse this financial variance scenario and generate a clear explanation suitable for the Board: "Revenue is up 4% versus budget, but net profit is down 2%. Interest income grew but fee income declined. Operating costs were over budget by 6% due to IT transformation spend." Ask Copilot to generate both a technical explanation and a plain-language Board narrative.

  2. Task 2: Use Copilot in Excel to create a variance analysis table template with columns: Business Unit, Metric, Budget, Actual, Variance (MYR), Variance (%), Commentary. Populate it with 5 sample rows and use Copilot to generate the Commentary column automatically.

  3. Task 3: Use Copilot in PowerPoint to create a 3-slide CFO Board summary showing: key financial highlights, variance explanations, and recommended actions. Focus on narrative clarity, not just numbers.

🎲 Game Master Guide

This scenario works best with Copilot in Excel (variance and bridge analysis), Copilot Chat (pattern recognition and narrative generation), and Copilot in PowerPoint (building a clear narrative arc). The stretch goal is Copilot Agent Builder — an agent that traces changes and explains the assumptions behind each variance. The Power Ups "Turn into KPIs Table", "Reframe for C-Suite", and "Next 90 Days Plan" are most powerful for this team.

⚡ Your Power Ups

These are prompt modifiers. Take your best Copilot output from the challenge and apply one (or more) of these to make it even stronger.

01 Turn into KPIs Table
"Convert the financial insights from your variance analysis into a KPI dashboard table: metric name, owner, current performance, target, trend (up/down/stable), and action required."

02 Reframe for C-Suite
"Rewrite your Board narrative as a one-slide style summary for the CEO — 3 bullet points maximum, focused on decisions, not details."
Apply this to: your PowerPoint Board summary slide to sharpen the executive message.
03 Check for Blind Spots
"Ask Copilot: what financial risks or emerging issues might not be visible in this month's numbers? What early warning indicators should the CFO be watching?"

"Based on the variance analysis, what should the Finance team prioritise in the next 90 days to improve reporting speed and quality? Give a phased plan."
Apply this to: your CFO Board summary to turn insights into a forward-looking action agenda.
